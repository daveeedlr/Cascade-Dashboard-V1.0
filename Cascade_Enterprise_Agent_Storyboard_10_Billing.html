Storyboard 10 —
Billing · deploy to invoice

The last stretch of a deployment: turning a finished job into an invoice. Sword pulls the hours from UKG, adds the contract-driven billables — hotels, rental cars via Corpay — applies the bill rates, and hands a finance coordinator a ready-to-review invoice instead of a spreadsheet stitched from four systems.

Prepared byDavid De los Reyes
ForJohn Cuellar
DateJul 17, 2026
UserDana · Billing
ScopePhase 3 · deploy-to-invoice
Product nameSword

Invoice assembly is where the money is actually made, and today it is manual: someone reads UKG time, chases the hotel folios, exports the Corpay car charges, and matches each line to a contract rate. The custom UKG time bridge is already built; this board adds the contract-driven billables beside it and assembles the whole invoice in NetSuite. Two things make this board matter. It catches the charges that should not bill — the terminated worker whose hotel kept running — before they reach the customer. And it keeps a human on the invoice, because the number that goes to a client is not something to guess.

Frame note

This is the three-month-hotel failure caught on the billing side — even if travel wasn't closed in time, the charge doesn't reach the customer.

  • It splits the folio by the worker's employment status, so the billable and non-billable nights are obvious.
  • The default is to exclude and flag, but "bill it — contract allows wind-down" exists, because sometimes the contract does. The human knows which.
  • Cross-references Storyboard 06 by name and date — the two boards describe the same worker from two desks.

Frame note

The same approval gate as every board, on the number that reaches a customer. It stops one step short of sending — posting a draft is reversible, sending an invoice is not.

  • The exclusion is shown as a line in the math, so the approver sees exactly what changed and why.
  • "Not sent to the client" is the deliberate boundary — the agent assembles and posts, a person releases.
  • The source trace makes the invoice auditable to its four systems, same discipline as the reconciliation board.

Frame note

"One screen showing the whole pilot job" — extended to billing. Invoice, cost, margin and the full trail, in one place.

  • Margin on the same screen as the invoice gives the account owner something they never had at close: the answer to "did we make money on this job?"
  • 1 day vs ~2 weeks is the measured win against the manual baseline.
  • The audit trail separates machine assembly from human approval — the same accountability model as reconciliation and the deploy run.

What this board adds

This closes the Huffmaster arc: order (08) → deploy (06) → invoice (10), one continuous flow across a recruiter, an operations lead, and a billing coordinator, all inside one Sword. It also lands the failure mode the whole engagement was pitched on, from the money side — the terminated worker's hotel is caught before it bills the client, not three months later. And it keeps the discipline the reconciliation board established: assembled by machine from named sources, approved by a person, posted with an audit trail, and never sent to a customer without a human releasing it.

Components this adds

  • Ready-to-bill queue
  • Itemized invoice with per-line source
  • Off-contract exception (folio by employment status)
  • Post-to-NetSuite approval gate
  • Margin + audit summary
