Field rep · WhatsApp only

This board carries the most commercial risk in the whole set. The entire commercial layer depends on reps answering a message while standing in a hardware store with a customer waiting — and Francisco already told us the honest truth about the last system: "the person that has to fill the information, he doesn't like it." If the thread is annoying, they stop replying, the data dies, and every dashboard in boards 01 through 04 goes empty. So the design problem here is not features. It is how few seconds this costs a busy person, and what happens when they ignore it anyway.

Two questions. That's the whole product. No form, no app, no login. The trigger is the visit ending, not a fixed clock — so it arrives when he is already walking back to the truck.
Frame note

Everything here is designed to lower the cost of answering. That is the only metric that matters on this surface.

  • Two questions, never more. Each extra one measurably drops reply rate.
  • Tap-to-answer chips for the first turn, so the cheapest reply is one thumb.
  • "Contéstame como quieras, yo lo ordeno" — the agent takes on the structuring work, not the rep. That sentence is the deal.
  • Fires on the visit-ended trigger from the listener in Storyboard 02.

Why this matters

Frame note

  • The agent reads back what it understood and asks one yes/no. Cheap to confirm, cheap to fix.

Escalation rule

One follow-up, four hours later, offering an easier format (voice note). Then silence.

If a rep's reply rate drops below 60% over two weeks, it surfaces on the sales manager's screen as a coaching item — not as an automated warning to the rep.

Frame note

The failure mode of every field-reporting tool ever built is nagging. This frame designs the restraint explicitly.

  • One nudge maximum. Then the agent shuts up and records what it does know.
  • The visit still counts — GPS confirmed he was there. Partial data is data.
  • Offering a voice note lowers the cost again for someone who hates typing.
  • Persistent silence becomes a human conversation with a manager, never a bot scolding a grown adult. This is the line that keeps reps using it.

Frame note

  • Leads with his own numbers, which is more persuasive than a target.
  • Mentions commission — motivation in his terms, not the company's.
  • Commits to following up Friday, which creates the accountability loop without a manager in the middle.

Frame note

The single most important adoption mechanic on this board: the channel has to be useful to the rep, not only to the company.

  • A read-only question — the "no" branch of the writes-outside gate. Instant answer, no approval needed.
  • It closes his sale and generates a reservation and an alert to Panchito, linking straight into Storyboard 03.
  • Every question like this makes the next reporting prompt more likely to get answered. Reciprocity as design.
05

The boundary — what the rep never sees, and what we never collect

TrustAdoption risk
design boundary · field rep role

What Luis gets

  • One WhatsApp thread, in Spanish, on the phone he already owns
  • A prompt after each visit — two questions
  • Live stock, price and delivery answers on demand
  • Coaching tips based on his own numbers
  • Confirmation of what was recorded, and the ability to correct it

What he never gets — by design

  • No login, no app, no password. Zero seats to buy, zero training
  • No dashboard. He never sees a leaderboard or another rep's numbers
  • No automated warnings. Underperformance goes to a human manager, never to a bot message
  • No continuous GPS. Location is captured at the moment he confirms a visit — the agent does not follow the truck around
  • No access to margins, costs, or other reps' accounts
Frame note

Francisco's ask was "I want to know where the sellers are." Answered literally, that builds a surveillance tool, and reps kill surveillance tools by quietly not replying.

  • Location is event-based, not continuous — it proves the visit happened without tracking a person's day.
  • No leaderboard is a deliberate choice. Ranking demotivates the bottom half, which is exactly the half we need to change.
  • Naming what we don't collect is the thing that gets reps to opt in. Worth saying out loud to Francisco before launch.
  • This frame is also the answer when a rep asks "¿me están vigilando?" — and someone will.

What this board settles

The commercial layer in boards 01 and 04 is only as good as this thread. Three rules fall out of it. Two questions, never more — every extra one costs reply rate. One nudge, then silence — nagging is how these systems die, and persistent non-response is a manager's job, not a bot's. And the channel must give before it takes: the stock question in Frame 04 is what buys the reporting answer in Frame 02. The privacy boundary in Frame 05 is not a compliance footnote — it is the adoption strategy.
