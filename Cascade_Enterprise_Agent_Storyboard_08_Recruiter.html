Storyboard 08 —
Recruiter · order to recruit

Storyboard 06 followed a deployment once people were hired. This one is upstream: the moment a staffing order lands and Sword turns it into open jobs, candidate pipelines, and — when a 700-person surge hits with ten days notice — an honest read of whether the team can fill it in time.

Prepared byDavid De los Reyes
ForJohn Cuellar

UserMarcus · Recruiter
ScopePhase 2 · order-to-recruit
Product nameSword

Huffmaster's Phase 2 thread: a Zoho Projects staffing order should auto-fire a Bullhorn job with the proforma data, and the recruiter should see openings and progress in one place instead of chasing spreadsheets. The function exists today — it just needs the trigger and the payload. The design test on this board is tempo: the same screens have to work for a routine 25-person order and for the 600-to-700 healthcare surge, sometimes with ten days notice, sometimes less. Triggers and queues, not humans in the loop — with the human stepping in exactly where judgement is needed.

Frame note

The temptation at surge tempo is to let the automation guess. This frame does the opposite — a job created with a wrong license type is worse than one held for an hour.

  • It names exactly which fields and why each matters — license for matching, rate for the offer and invoice.

The honest read not a promise

At the current outreach-to-accept rate, Sword projects 560 of 700 filled by the start date — a 140-seat gap. It is not going to pretend otherwise. Three levers can close it, each with a cost.

Which levers do you want to pull?
+90Open outreach to the adjacent region (2h drive)
+40Raise the bill rate 8% to lift accept rate
+30Re-engage 220 lapsed candidates with a bonus
Apply levers 1 & 3 Model a different mix rate change needs manager sign-off
Frame note

The proposal's hardest promise: the same workflow holds at 45 and at 700. The screens are identical — what changes is that the agent has to be honest about a gap it can't automate away.

  • It projects a shortfall and says so, rather than showing a green bar that lies. Trust is built on the 140.
  • Each lever is priced in seats, and the rate change is correctly routed to a manager's approval — decisions land with the right role.
  • This is judgement, not automation: the agent lays out options, a human chooses the mix.

Frame note

The point of this frame is the seam that isn't there. Recruiting and operations are one flow, so the storyboard hands off exactly where the work does.

  • This is the same run id (h2d:8812) that opens Storyboard 06 — the two boards are literally the same object seen from two roles.
  • No re-keying, no "please notify ops" email — the failure mode this whole engagement exists to kill.
  • It shows how one platform serves a recruiter and an operations lead without either leaving their view.

What this board adds

Storyboard 06 proved the platform claim across industries; this one proves it across roles inside one tenant. A recruiter and an operations lead use the same Sword, see different doors, and pass work between them with no handoff. It also faces the hardest promise in the Huffmaster proposal head-on: at a 700-person surge the agent does not fake a green bar — it projects the 140-seat gap, prices the ways to close it, and routes the rate decision to the person who owns it. Honest math is the feature.
