Content Pipeline

Another vertical, and the one Cascade runs on itself. The agent watches for content signals — a post that landed, a theme from a call — drafts in the brand voice, designs a branded asset straight from the brand manual, and pushes approved pieces to Slack, the newsletter and the blog. A brand-and-voice check stands between draft and publish.

The Content Pipeline captures signals from LinkedIn, email and the web, designs branded assets via Pencil straight from the brand manual, and auto-pushes approved pieces to Slack, the newsletter and the blog. It is the clearest demo of a hard idea: an agent can be creative and stay on-brand, because the brand is encoded — the palette, the type, the voice rules, Clara — and a Voice & Brand check gates every piece. As with every other board, the agent drafts and designs; a human approves before anything is published.

Draft · "Agents propose, humans approve"

Everyone asks what stops an AI agent from doing something reckless.

Our answer is boring on purpose: the agent proposes, a human approves. Nothing that touches your systems — a CRM update, an invoice, a message to a customer — happens until a person signs off.

That one rule is why teams trust it with real work. Not magic. A gate.

Frame note

The keystone of the vertical, and the reason a client trusts an agent with their brand: a gate that can say no to your own content.

  • It passes voice, tokens and style — then holds on an unverifiable claim, which is the failure mode that actually embarrasses a brand.
  • It doesn't just block; it proposes a defensible rewrite of equal strength.
  • This is David's real Voice & Brand agent doing the job it was built for — the linter as a publish gate.
  • Nothing moves to Frame 05 until this passes.

Frame note

Publishing is the outward action, so it's an approval gate like every other write in the platform. The agent got the piece ready; a person releases it.

  • Channel by channel: Slack now, newsletter queued, blog published — each with its own reversibility stated.
  • LinkedIn company page is deliberately manual — some channels want a human hand, and the board respects that.
  • Same run-and-audit pattern as every board: signal → draft → design → check → approve → publish, all logged.

What this board proves

Reconciliation showed the platform can do exact, auditable work. This vertical shows it can do creative work without going off the rails — because the brand is encoded, not hoped for. Signals the business already generates become on-brand drafts and assets, a Voice & Brand gate catches the off-brand claim before anyone sees it, and a person approves the publish. It's the vertical Cascade runs on itself, which makes it the most honest demo in the set: the agent that markets Cascade is held to the same "propose, then a human approves" rule as the agent that reconciles a client's ledger.
