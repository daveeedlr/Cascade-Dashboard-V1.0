Enterprise Reconciliation

A different job entirely. Same shell, same gate, a whole new vertical.

Reconciliation is the most developed Cascade vertical and the one behind John's live demo. The job: continuously compare payroll, tax and GL against the accounting system, surface every discrepancy, and propose a correction for a human to approve. Two claims make it different from the pipeline boards. First, the money math is done by deterministic tools, never guessed by the model — reproducible to the cent. Second, every proposed fix is staged for sign-off with a full audit trail. This board is tagged Cascade · Platform, not a client, because it is the vertical itself, ready to re-skin onto whichever brand the demo needs.

Branch 04b — reject with reason

If the controller knows the GL rate was correct and payroll double-counted a differential, she rejects: "Payroll error, not GL — route to payroll to reverse." The entry is not posted, the reason is saved, and the agent learns this pay group's differential rule for next close.

Reject & route to payroll→ knowledge model
Frame note

The same approval gate as every other board, applied to money. It states the consequence before asking and never posts on its own.

  • The proposed fix is shown as a balanced journal entry a controller can read at a glance — debits equal credits.
  • Reject-with-reason is a real path: the agent might be right about the delta but wrong about the fix, and the human decides.
  • A rejection teaches the knowledge model the pay group's rule, so the same discrepancy resolves itself next month.
  • Every action is signed and logged — the audit trail Frame 05 shows.

Why an auditor trusts this

Every figure was computed by a deterministic tool and can be re-run to the identical cent. Every action names who did it and when. The AI found and explained the problem; a named human approved the money movement; the system posted it. Nothing in the chain is a guess.

Frame note

The vertical's closing argument, and the reason it's the most developed one: finance can't run on "probably."

  • The trail separates machine detection from human approval — the auditable boundary the architecture diagram draws.
  • "Reproducible to the identical cent" is what makes this safe to sell into a controller's close process.
  • Same run/audit pattern as the Construgypsum CRM run and the Huffmaster deploy — one platform, one accountability model.

What this board proves

Eight boards moved work through pipelines; this one does something categorically different — it checks numbers and moves money — and it still runs on the same shell, the same list-detail, the same approval gate. That is the strongest form of the platform claim: not just a new tenant, but a new vertical, built from the parts already on screen. Two things make reconciliation the one to lead a demo with: the math is deterministic and reproducible to the cent, and every correction is staged for a named human with a full audit trail. It's tagged Cascade · Platform because it isn't a client — it's a capability, ready to wear whichever brand the pitch calls for.
