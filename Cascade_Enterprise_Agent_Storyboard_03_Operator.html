Storyboard 03 —
Operator · inventory

A second user, as John asked. Panchito runs inventory at Chayabamba. He is not an admin, he does not care about connectors, and most days he never opens the app at all — the agent reaches him on WhatsApp. This board follows one stock-out from the message on his phone to a purchase he approves.

Prepared byDavid De los Reyes
ForJohn Cuellar
DateJul 17, 2026
UserPanchito · Operator
ScopePhase 1 · Ticket 2
Versionv0.1 · draft

This is the quick win we are actually selling — the Chayabamba inventory automation, weeks one to four. It matters as a storyboard because it breaks an assumption the first two boards carried: that the user comes to the app. Panchito's day starts in WhatsApp, and the workspace is somewhere he goes only when the message tells him something needs a decision. Design the entry point outside the product, or the product does not get used.

Frame note

The most important screen in this board is not in the app. If the digest is wrong, too long, or in the wrong language, nothing downstream matters.

  • Written in Spanish, short, ranked worst-first — three severity tiers, not a wall of SKUs.
  • It names a pattern ("third time this quarter") — that is the analyst's judgement, not a raw alert.
  • One deep link in. Everything else can wait.
  • This is the run the admin approved at the end of Storyboard 02.

Masilla Intaco 20kg — diagnóstico

El proveedor entregó normal las últimas 3 órdenes, sin demoras ni alzas. La última orden de compra fue el 9 jun, para 120 unidades — por debajo del buffer de 190. Es una falla de compra, no del proveedor.

Frame note

This frame exists because the proposal promises it: separate supplier-driven gaps (tariffs, freight) from purchasing failures. It is the difference between a report and an opinion.

  • Two boxes, two colours, two different follow-up conversations.
  • The agent commits to a call — "falla de compra, no del proveedor" — with the evidence beside it.
  • This is the screen that makes Francisco trust or distrust the system, which is why Frame 06 exists.

  • The transfer costs $0 and is listed first. The agent solves it the cheap way before spending money.
  • "Draft POs, nothing sent to the supplier" — consequences stated before asking.
  • Branch 05b drawn in place: over the limit, approval becomes escalation, never a dead end.

Frame note

This is the frame most products never draw: the agent being wrong, on purpose, in public. It comes straight from the Yale/deadbolt false flag Francisco caught on the Jul 9 call.

  • The correction is captured as a rule, not a one-off fix — "all new lines under 6 months."
  • That is the knowledge model being built in front of the client, which is what we actually sell.
  • Note the avatar changed: this session is Francisco's, not Panchito's.
  • A weekly 15-minute ritual is a design decision as much as a project plan.

What this board settles

Three things worth arguing about before anyone builds. The entry point is WhatsApp, not the app — so the digest is a first-class designed artifact with its own copy rules. Roles reshape the product, not just the permissions: Panchito's app has four doors and speaks Spanish. And being wrong is a designed state — the weekly correction loop is where the knowledge model gets built, which is the thing we are actually selling.
